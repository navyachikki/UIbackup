The SC was reportedly hearing the case where it sought a reply from the government-run State Bank of India (SBI) as to why electoral bond numbers were not disclosed.
The case was heard by a five-judge Constitution bench — comprising CJI DY Chandrachud and justices Sanjiv Khanna, BR Gavai, JB Pardiwala and Manoj Misra — on Monday.
The SC told the SBI to stop being “selective” and make “complete disclosure” of all details related to the electoral bonds scheme by March 21.
